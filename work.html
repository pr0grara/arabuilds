A few local service businesses I've put online. Every build runs the same playbook: loads fast, says the offer up front, dead easy to contact.

One thing worth saying before you click: these are unbranded and plain on purpose. Depending on the trade, that's what converts. Someone searching garage door repair at 9pm isn't after a glossy brand, they want a local who'll pick up, and a page that looks like the contractor threw it together himself often books more jobs than a polished one. The rough is tuned to the trade.

Under that plain surface is the part that actually matters: SEO built to get found, a service-area page for every town you cover. Content written for your business, not a cookie-cutter template with your name swapped in. Genuinely useful info a customer wants to read, not filler. And it's all structured so AI search and crawlers can read it, so you show up when people ask an assistant who to call.
